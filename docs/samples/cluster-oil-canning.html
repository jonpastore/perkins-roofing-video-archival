What is oil canning and why does it happen?

Oil canning occurs when a perfectly flat metal panel reflects sunlight and water, creating a rippling effect that looks like dents.

  • Flat standing‑seam panels have no ribs or striations to break up the reflection.
  • The effect is strongest on panels with a smooth surface and a low slope.
  • It’s an optical illusion, not a structural problem.

How can striated panels reduce oil canning?

Striated or double‑ribbed panels break up light reflections, so the illusion is far less noticeable.

  • Tim shows striated panels on his roof and says they “help against seeing the oil canning.”
  • The ribs create shadows that mask the rippling effect.
  • Most South Florida installations use striated panels for this reason.

When should I ask my roofer about oil canning?

You should discuss oil canning during the estimate if you’re considering flat standing‑seam panels for a low‑slope roof.

  1. Ask if the contractor will install striated or double‑ribbed panels.
  2. Confirm the panel profile (flat vs. ribbed) before signing the contract.
  3. Request a visual sample of the chosen panel on site.

Why does oil canning matter for warranty coverage?

Oil canning itself isn’t covered under warranty because it’s a visual effect, not a defect.

Tim explains that warranties cover “specifications only” and “damage caused by other circumstances” is excluded. Since oil canning isn’t a physical defect, it doesn’t trigger warranty work.

FAQ

  • Does oil canning affect roof performance? No. It’s only an optical illusion; the roof’s water barrier remains intact.
  • Can I fix oil canning after installation? The only fix is to replace flat panels with ribbed ones; there’s no coating that eliminates the effect.
  • Is oil canning covered by insurance? No, because it isn’t a leak or structural damage.
  • Will oil canning show up in home inspections? Inspectors note it as a visual characteristic, not a defect.
  • Do all metal roof manufacturers offer striated panels? Most major suppliers do; Tim’s supplier GF Coast Supply provides both flat and striated options.
